A blended report places metrics from different systems into a single view, aligned on a shared dimension.

<Info>**Status:** ❌ Not yet composable for truly unrelated tables — works when sources share a joinable dimension.</Info>

## The question

> "Show me marketing spend vs revenue by channel for last quarter."

## Why it matters

Marketing spend lives in your ad platform data. Revenue lives in your orders table. Executives want both in one chart to calculate ROI by channel. If the two tables share a join path — even through an intermediate dimension table — Lightdash can combine them. If they share nothing at all, you need a workaround.

## Example

A SaaS company tracks ad spend and order revenue across the same three acquisition channels.

### Source data

**marketing_spend**

| spend_id | channel_id | amount |
|----------|------------|--------|
| 1 | 10 | 4,200 |
| 2 | 20 | 7,800 |
| 3 | 30 | 2,500 |

**orders**

| order_id | channel_id | order_total |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| 501 | 10 | 12,400 |
| 502 | 10 | 8,300 |
| 503 | 20 | 15,600 |
| 504 | 30 | 6,100 |

**channels**

| channel_id | channel_name |
|------------|--------------|
| 10 | Paid Search |
| 20 | Social |
| 30 | Email |

### Desired result

| channel_name | total_spend | total_revenue | ROI |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|------|
| Paid Search | $4,200 | $20,700 | 4.9× |
| Social | $7,800 | $15,600 | 2.0× |
| Email | $2,500 | $6,100 | 2.4× |

### YAML

Because both fact tables join to `channels`, Lightdash can blend them with symmetric aggregates:

```yaml
version: 2
models:
  - name: channels
    meta:
      primary_key: channel_id
      joins:
        - join: marketing_spend
          sql_on: ${channels.channel_id} = ${marketing_spend.channel_id}
          relationship: one-to-many
        - join: orders
          sql_on: ${channels.channel_id} = ${orders.channel_id}
          relationship: one-to-many
    columns:
      - name: channel_name
        meta:
          dimension:
            type: string

  - name: marketing_spend
    meta:
      primary_key: spend_id
    columns:
      - name: amount
        meta:
          metrics:
            total_spend:
              type: sum
              format: "usd"

  - name: orders
    meta:
      primary_key: order_id
    columns:
      - name: order_total
        meta:
          metrics:
            total_revenue:
              type: sum
              format: "usd"
```

### Result

Explore `channels`, select `channel_name`, `total_spend`, and `total_revenue`. Lightdash aggregates each fact table in its own CTE — no fan-out.

## How to set it up

1. Identify a shared dimension both sources can join to (here, `channels`).
2. Declare `primary_key` on every model and `relationship: one-to-many` on the joins.
3. Explore from the shared dimension table and select metrics from both fact tables.
4. Add a derived metric for ROI if needed: `sql: ${total_revenue} / NULLIF(${total_spend}, 0)`.

## Workaround: no shared dimension

If the two sources share no join path at all, Lightdash cannot combine them in a single Explore query today. Pre-join in dbt instead:

```sql
SELECT
  COALESCE(s.channel, r.channel) AS channel,
  s.total_spend,
  r.total_revenue
FROM {{ ref('spend_by_channel') }} s
FULL OUTER JOIN {{ ref('revenue_by_channel') }} r
  ON s.channel = r.channel
```

Define this staging model in Lightdash and explore it directly. Merged queries across unrelated Explores are on the roadmap.
