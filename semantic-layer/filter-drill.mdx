Analysis rarely stops at the first chart. The Filter Drill is the pattern of narrowing: start broad, spot an outlier, click to zoom in.

<Info>**Status:** ✅ Fully supported.</Info>

## The question

> "Revenue by region — then drill into EMEA by city, then by sales rep."

## Why it matters

A top-level number hides the story. EMEA might look flat overall while Berlin is surging and Paris is declining. Without the ability to drill, analysts either build dozens of pre-filtered charts or field ad-hoc requests all day. Cross-filtering lets anyone self-serve the follow-up question.

## Example

An online store with sales reps across regions and cities.

### Source data

| order_id | region | city | sales_rep | order_total |
|----------|--------|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1001 | EMEA | Berlin | Klaus M. | 4,200 |
| 1002 | EMEA | Paris | Sophie L. | 1,800 |
| 1003 | EMEA | Berlin | Anna W. | 6,100 |
| 1004 | APAC | Tokyo | Yuki T. | 3,400 |
| 1005 | EMEA | Paris | Sophie L. | 2,900 |
| 1006 | AMER | New York | Jake R. | 5,500 |

### YAML

```yaml
version: 2
models:
  - name: orders
    columns:
      - name: region
        meta:
          dimension:
            type: string
      - name: city
        meta:
          dimension:
            type: string
      - name: sales_rep
        meta:
          dimension:
            type: string
      - name: order_total
        meta:
          metrics:
            total_revenue:
              type: sum
              format: "usd"
```

### Result

**Step 1 — by region:**

| region | total_revenue |
|--------|---------------|
| EMEA | $218,400 |
| AMER | $194,600 |
| APAC | $87,300 |

**Step 2 — filter to EMEA, group by city:**

| city | total_revenue |
|------|---------------|
| Berlin | $92,100 |
| London | $61,800 |
| Paris | $44,200 |

**Step 3 — filter to Berlin, group by sales rep:**

| sales_rep | total_revenue |
|-----------|---------------|
| Anna W. | $51,300 |
| Klaus M. | $40,800 |

## How to set it up

1. Define dimensions at each level of your hierarchy (region, city, sales_rep).
2. Build a dashboard chart showing the top-level roll-up (revenue by region).
3. Enable **cross-filtering** on the dashboard — clicking "EMEA" filters all tiles.
4. Add a second tile grouped by city; it responds to the region filter automatically.
5. Use **Drill into** on any chart bar to jump to the underlying rows or a finer dimension.

## Tips

- Cross-filtering works across all tiles on a dashboard, not just the chart you click.
- Combine with date filters to drill within a specific time window.
